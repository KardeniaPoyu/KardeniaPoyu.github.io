自己紹介

About Me

中央民族大学にて情報と計算科学を専攻している学部生です。 数値計算・線形代数・確率統計を中心とした数理的基礎を背景に、 大規模言語モデル（LLM）および強化学習（RL）に基づく 学習アルゴリズムと意思決定メカニズムに関心を持っています。

これまでの学習・研究を通じて、 学習手法そのものの設計や最適化プロセスに強い関心を抱くようになり、 現在は LLM の強化学習補助型微調整や方策最適化に代表される 学習アルゴリズム指向の研究へと関心を広げています。

今、私は中国科学院自動化研究所にて研究助手（Research Assistant）として研究実習に参加し、 DeepSeek によって提案された RPO / GRPO （Reinforcement Learning-based Preference Optimization）をはじめとする強化学習に基づく LLM 微調整手法の研究に取り組んでいます。 特に、車のインタネット（Internet of Vehicles, IoV）におけるネットワークレベルの移動性予測問題を対象とし、 通信資源管理やネットワーク意思決定を支援するための 学習手法の適用および改良を行っています。

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Information and Computing Science at Minzu University of China. With a solid mathematical background in numerical analysis, linear algebra, and probability, my interests lie in learning algorithms and decision-making mechanisms based on large language models (LLMs) and reinforcement learning (RL).

Through my academic training and research experience, I have gradually shifted my focus toward algorithm-oriented research, particularly reinforcement learning–assisted fine-tuning and policy optimization for LLMs, rather than application-specific modeling alone.

Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant at the Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CASIA), where I study reinforcement learning–based fine-tuning methods such as RPO and GRPO (Reinforcement Learning-based Preference Optimization). My research focuses on applying and extending these methods to network-level mobility prediction problems in the Internet of Vehicles (IoV), aiming to support communication resource management and adaptive network decision-making.

I am currently working as a Research Assistant at the Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CASIA), focusing on reinforcement learning–based fine-tuning mechanisms for large language models, with particular emphasis on DeepSeek-style RPO and GRPO methods. This research investigates the application and extension of GRPO (Reinforcement Preference Optimization) to network-oriented mobility prediction problems in the Internet of Vehicles (IoV). Specifically, the target task is mobility prediction designed to support network-level decision-making, such as communication resource allocation in dynamic vehicular networks.
  • Reproduction and implementation of DeepSeek RPO / GRPO for LLM fine-tuning
  • Systematic review of highly-cited network-level mobility prediction literature in IoV
  • Application of GRPO to mobility prediction for network decision support and comparison with established baseline methods
  • Empirical analysis of training stability, efficiency, and generalization
  • Design of task-specific enhancements to GRPO based on observed limitations and networking characteristics

Conducted research on improving YOLOv8-based vehicle detection models for complex urban scenarios. Implemented and evaluated the following approaches to enhance detection accuracy:
  • Integration of attention mechanisms to improve model performance
  • Mosaic augmentation strategy to enhance training data diversity
  • Heatmap-based interpretability analysis for visualization of detection behavior
  • Trade-off analysis between computational efficiency and accuracy for real-time processing
This work will be presented at the Conference on Computer Vision and Image Processing (CVIP) 2025.

研究関心

Research Interests

私の研究関心は、大規模言語モデル（LLM）と強化学習を基盤とした マルチモーダル知能エージェントの設計と学習メカニズムにあります。 視覚・言語・環境状態などの異種情報を統合し、 推論・意思決定・行動生成を一貫して行うエージェントシステムに強い関心を持っています。

これまでのコンピュータビジョン分野での研究経験を通じて、 視覚情報が意思決定や行動選択にどのように寄与するかという課題意識を持つようになりました。 現在はこの視点を発展させ、 視覚入力を含むマルチモーダル情報を LLM による高次推論へ接続し、 強化学習によって行動方策を学習する知能エージェントの研究に取り組んでいます。

特に、以下の観点から理論と実装の両面に関心を持っています。

  • 視覚・言語・状態情報を統合したマルチモーダル表現学習
  • LLM を用いたマルチモーダル推論および意思決定（decision reasoning）
  • 強化学習による方策学習（policy learning）と行動最適化
  • ツール使用や外部モジュール連携を含むエージェント設計
  • 知覚・推論・行動を統合したエンドツーエンドな知能システム

My research interests focus on multimodal intelligent agents based on large language models (LLMs) and reinforcement learning. I am particularly interested in agent systems that integrate visual, linguistic, and state information for reasoning, decision-making, and action generation.

Through my previous work in computer vision, I developed a strong interest in how visual perception can be connected to downstream reasoning and control. Building on this foundation, I now study multimodal agents that combine high-level reasoning with policy learning in complex environments.

My current research interests include:

  • Multimodal representation learning with vision, language, and state inputs
  • Multimodal decision reasoning and planning with large language models
  • Policy learning and adaptation via reinforcement learning
  • Agent architectures with tool use and external module integration
  • End-to-end multimodal agents integrating perception, reasoning, and action
